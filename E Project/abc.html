HIKING

Hiking is a long, vigorous walk, usually on trails or footpaths in the countryside. Walking for pleasure developed in Europe during the eighteenth century. Young children are prone to becoming fatigued more rapidly than adults, requiring fluids and energy-rich foods more frequently, and are also more sensitive to variations in weather and terrain. Hiking routes may be chosen with these factors in mind, and appropriate clothing, equipment and sun-protection need to be available. MAK Alpinism provides their travelers an opportunity to learn hiking and its techniques. The one who loves to walk through bushes, mountains, hills and rocks. MAK Alpinism educate their learners an ethic way of hiking with the use of required equipments and safety precautions.

Margala Hills Margalla Hills National Park on the northern edge of Islamabad Capital Territory, Pakistan,
just south of Haripur District, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. They are part of the Himalayan foothills.
The Margalla range has an area of 12,605 hectares .There are several hiking trails in the Margalla Hills.
Trail 3 and 5 are the most popular ones, starting from the Margalla Road opposite sector F-6 and ending at the Pir Sohawa Road		 3- 3.5 hours of hike
2 hours of stay.
Miranjani The Miranjani trek starts an hour of walk from the Governor House in Nathia Gali.
The total length of the track is about 4.69 km and a net elevation of about 600 meters . The path to the peak is
canopied with tall trees and is abundant in greenery.Nathia Gali, Mushkpuri Peak and Azad Kashmir are easily visible from the top.
On a clear day even Nanga Parbat, the ninth highest mountain in the world, at a displacement of 166 km or a distance of more than
400 km can be seen shining with its snow clad peaks.		 5 hours approx to hike
3 hours of stay and camp
Dunga gali Ayubia Hike to a magnificant Pipeline Track reaching Dunga Gali from Ayubia. A track of 4km full
of maginifcant and lush scenery making your trip a memorable one ! Dungan Gali pipeline track follows
the pipeline from Donga Gali to Ayubia,a distance of 3.5 kilometers, having altitude around 8,200 ft. You are urged to capture
unforgettable memories, natural beauty and charming weather enjoying nature miracles like rain, sunshine, fog, and clouds in a single moment.		 40 to 50mins of hike
40mins of stay and rest
Naran to Lake Saif-ul-Muluk Naran Kaghan is famous as a tourist destination, owing to its pleasant weather in peak season every year thousands of tourists rush to explore the valley. It is also the gateway to Gilgit Hunza. You could not have chosen a better place for your trekking hiking adventure when it comes to the wild organic beauty of the lakes, mountains and passes in this valley. It is located 119 kilometers (74 mi) from Mansehra city at the altitude of 2,409 meters (7,904 ft). It is located about 65 kilometers (40 mi) away from Babusar Top. Best time to hike is from month of June to September. , 2 Days of hiking with 3 hours of break and camp.
Hunza Valley Hunza Valley is a beautiful place and home to some of the best hiking
and trekking in the world.Hunza Valley is a remarkably peaceful and beautiful place
situated at an elevation of 2500 meters, it is situated 112 km north of Gilgit on the west bank of the Hunza river. The hiking trek starts from Hunza Patundass, Shatubar Glacier, Yengutz peak, Batura Glacier, Shimshal Pass , Hunza Rush Lake, Diran Peak, Chafchingol Pass, Batura Round treks and finally ends with Rakaposhi base camp. 		3 to 4 Days
With 2,2 hours of rest and camp
Kel to Chitta Katha Lake Chitta Katha lake is a wonderful lake in the Shounter valley of Azad Kashmir. The hiking trek starts along-with a 5 kilometers (3.1 miles) long lush green beautiful patch. Kel is the base-camp for the lake. Its altitude is 13,500 feet (4,115 meters) above sea level.A trek that starts from Kel to Shounter Vallery ,Domail Valley , Sarwali Peak and Hari Parbat, Nangi Mali Mountain, Dak-1, Dak-2 and then finally reach to Chitta Katha Lake. 14 to 16 hours with camp and stay.
